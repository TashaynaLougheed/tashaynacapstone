ABOUT N'QUATQUA

N'Quatqua Transtion House is located on the unceded Territory of N'Quatqua First Nations, also known as D'arcy,of the Stl'at'imx Territory - Interior Salish people. N"Quatqua is in the Squamish - Lillooet region of Southwestern British Columbia on the Southern Tip of Anderson Lake. Approximately 160km North of Vancouver. The population of N'Quatqua is approximately 300 members.

PROGRAM CRITERIA

Transition House program criteria for those who choose to seek services or shelter are:SINGLE WOMEN or WOMEN WITH CHILDREN fleeing from abuse or domestic violence from an INTIMATE PARTNER

Domestic Violence Can Be Defined As:

  • Physical abuse
  • Emotional abuse
  • Spiritual abuse
  • Sexual abuse
  • Financial abuse
  • Economical abuse
  • Psychological abuse
  • OR THREATS OF SUCH ABUSE

Intimate Partner Can Be Defined As: With respect to a person

  • Current or Former Spouse/Partner
  • Common Law Partner
  • Dating Partner

At Risk or Threat of Violence:

REFERS TO A SITUATION WHERE A WOMEN INDICATES THAT SHE AND/OR HER CHILDREN ARE AT RISK OF EXPERIENCING VIOLENCE

PROGRAM

House Capcity

The Transition House has 7 units, 3 of which are single occupancy with access to a shared/communal kitchen and bathroom. 4 family units that are fully self - contained, with the exception of a shared laundry room. The capicty of the transition house is 18 - 23 people.

Length of Stay

The length of stay at a transition house is typically 30 days, however we recognize the unique needs of the communities, therefor, the length of stay at this transition house will vary, depending on the residents' needs.

Resident Expectations

While residents are here they will recieve basic life skills, advocacy, and support. We encourage residents to be as independant as possible during their stay here. All house rules must be followed and limit to personal socializing, which is to encourage residents to work on the issues that brought them to the house.

Program Mission

The mission of the transition house is to reduce barriers and create accessible safe services for all women and children in need of support

Program Goals

The goal of the transition house is to support women and thier children while they make the difficult transition from an abusive or violent domestic enviroment to a positive, independant, violent free lifestyle

Program Values

We value those that are dealing with hardship and currently living in a difficult situation. We believe every women and child should have a choice in decisions about thier care, and that they have the right to a safe, respectful enviroment.
